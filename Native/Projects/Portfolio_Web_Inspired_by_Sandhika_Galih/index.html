About Me

As a licensed elementary educator with over 5 years of experience, I bring creativity, patience, and exceptional communication skills to the classroom. My innovative approach to lesson design has earned a 90% satisfaction rate among both students and colleagues. Beyond education, I have a keen interest in design, with hands-on experience in Canva, Figma, and CapCut. I have successfully led various projects for my startup and a local youth organization, where I am an active member and advocate for positive community impact.

My dedication to education extends to special initiatives, including programs focused on empowering individuals with disabilities and providing skill training. Recently, I've been expanding my expertise in technology, particularly in web development, to further enhance my teaching methods. My goal is to integrate game-based learning into my curriculum, making education more engaging through narrative-driven gamification. I am also developing an innovative evaluation tool called TOS (Tree of Studying) to better assess student progress.

My Projects

Education

Our two main learning media sources are Canva and Quizizz. With Canva, we can make learning materials more interactive, editable, and efficient, while also further honing our design skills. With Quizizz, children will enjoy the gamified teaching approach, making it easier to monitor both their results and evaluations. Additionally, it enables mentors to easily implement differentiated learning.

One of our flagship programs is the Literacy Festival. Through this program, it is hoped that children will become more proficient in reading and be able to create various art projects, both hands-on and digital, according to their individual interests and talents related to the books they have read. This will allow them to express their understanding based on their level of imagination and creativity.

The monthly evaluation activity is a face-to-face group assessment that is integrated with interactive and engaging technological resources through the Mentimer website. There, we reflect with each other—students and mentors alike—share snacks, and participate in various challenges. Through this activity, we hope to identify what we should maintain and improve based on this month's learning.
